About Me

Names are an integral part of a person’s identity, reflecting culture, history, and even family expectations of the individual. One interesting example of the journey of a name is the case of Indonesian President Joko Widodo, whose real name was Mulyono. Although widely known as Joko Widodo, the journey of the name Mulyono to Joko Widodo provides interesting insights into the changes in identity and background. Reporting from kompas.com, Jokowi is the first child and only son of four siblings, with three younger sisters. His father, Widjiatno Noto, is a carpenter, and his mother, Sujiatmi Notomiharjo, is a housewife. At birth, the baby was originally named Mulyono, not Joko Widodo. In his interview, Jokowi explained that the change from Mulyono to Joko Widodo occurred because he experienced various childhood health problems. The name Mulyono, which means “noble”, was considered inappropriate for the baby’s condition. Therefore, the name was changed to Joko Widodo which means “a boy who is safe and prosperous”. On the other hand, account X, which discussed threats related to Jokowi's real name, raised topics about occult or mystical knowledge related to shamanism and ancestral spirits.
